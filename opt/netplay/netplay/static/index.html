Bishops, The Game - Simple Rules (current build) as of January 14, 2026

Overview

Four players: White, Grey, Black, Pink. The board is 12x12 with a central 8x8 play area (the green frame). The four 2x2 corner blocks are out of play. Turns go clockwise: White, Grey, Black, Pink.

Setup and play

On your turn, you make one normal chess move with your own pieces. Any piece can capture any opposing color. Check and checkmate apply. You can put any king in check; you must get out of check on your turn. Castling is allowed in both directions. Pawns move and capture like chess. A pawn promotes when it reaches the far edge of the central 8x8 area in its direction of travel (the side opposite its start). King/Queen swap: On your first turn only, you may swap your king and queen. In the current program, the swap does NOT advance the turn, so you must still make a normal move to pass the turn.

Elimination and duel

When a player is checkmated or resigns, that color is eliminated and removed from the board. When only two colors remain, the game becomes a chess duel on the central 8x8 area.

Blocked corners (important)

The four 2x2 corner blocks are permanently off limits to all pieces. No piece may enter those squares.

Notes about current behavior

The program treats the King/Queen swap as a setup action, not a full move. You keep the turn after swapping and must make a regular move before play passes to the next color.
